Accessibility

jestamang.com

Our Commitment

Jestamang is committed to ensuring jestamang.com is accessible to all visitors, including those with disabilities. We are actively working toward conformance with WCAG 2.1 Level AA accessibility standards and will continue to improve the experience for every user of this site.

Measures We Have Taken

The following accessibility features are currently implemented across jestamang.com:

  • Descriptive alt text on images throughout the site
  • Keyboard navigable interface and navigation menu
  • High contrast color scheme — gold on dark background — for legibility
  • Luminari font at readable sizes with generous line spacing
  • Fully mobile responsive design across all pages
  • Labels on all form input fields
  • Cookie usage limited to Firebase session authentication, disclosed in our Privacy Policy
  • Semantic HTML elements (header, nav, main, article, footer) used throughout
  • Skip-to-content capability via keyboard navigation

Known Limitations

Our custom browser-based games may have limited accessibility for users relying on screen readers or keyboard-only navigation. These games involve real-time canvas rendering which presents inherent accessibility challenges. We are aware of this limitation and are actively working to improve it.

Some decorative visual elements, including animated backgrounds and layered CSS effects, are marked as presentational and do not affect content accessibility.

Compatibility

jestamang.com is designed and tested to work with the following:

  • Chrome, Firefox, Safari, and Edge — current versions
  • Screen readers: NVDA, JAWS, and VoiceOver
  • Mobile browsers: iOS Safari and Android Chrome
  • Zoom levels up to 200% without loss of content

Feedback

If you experience any accessibility barriers on jestamang.com, we want to hear from you. Please contact us via the email page on this site. We take all accessibility feedback seriously and will work to address reported issues promptly.

Enforcement

If you are located in the EU or UK and are unsatisfied with our response to an accessibility concern, you have the right to contact your local accessibility enforcement authority or supervisory body. In the UK this is the Equality and Human Rights Commission. In the EU, relevant enforcement bodies vary by member state.
